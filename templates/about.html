Karissa McKelvey

Karissa McKelvey is a software engineer by training, but now she spends most of her day talking to open source stakeholders and bringing them together. As the Director of the Dat Foundation, she works with diverse sets of engineers and product leaders, from environmentalists all the way to blockchain companies, who have realized that somewhere along the way, the web lost its bearings. She helps them succeed in their goals to respect the digital rights of their users. In 2016, she cofounded Code for Science and Society to systematize this work across the open source ecosystem of public interest technology. This community leads with ethics: human rights, universal access, security, privacy, and inclusion. These projects hinge on the fact that industry left alone will not meet the needs of the public interest. She realizes technology is never enough on its own to move the web in the right direction. To this end, she is currently a fellow at the Aspen Institute's Tech Policy Hub, where she is growing her skills to advocatie for digital human rights around the globe. Formerly a data scientist, her work studying online political communication resulted in multiple peer-reviewed papers and press in outlets such as NPR and the Wall Street Journal.
