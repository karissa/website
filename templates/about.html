Karissa McKelvey is a software engineer by training and a technology strategist by trade. She is a generalist who "connects the dots" between emerging technology. She helps non-profit organizations, companies, and small projects reach better outcomes for universal access, security, privacy, inclusion, and equity.

She is an expert on cooperative and sustainable practices in open source. In 2016, she co-founded Code for Science and Society with the aim to systematize this work across the ecosystem of open source public interest technology. As the Director of the Dat Foundation, she works with diverse sets of stakeholders who aim to build a better Web that values digital rights.

It's clear that technological solutions on their own is not enough to move the global Web in an ethical direction. To this end, she is currently a fellow at the Aspen Institute's Tech Policy Hub, where she is growing her skills to advocate for policies in the interest of digital human rights around the globe.

Formerly a data scientist, her work studying online political communication resulted in multiple peer-reviewed papers and press in outlets such as NPR and the Wall Street Journal.
