Karissa McKelvey is a software engineer by training, but now she spends most of her day consulting with open source engineers and project leaders, helping them achieve their goals. She is an expert on cooperative and sustainable practices in open source, particularly within a digital rights framework that encompasses universal access, security, privacy, inclusion, and equity.

As the Director of the Dat Foundation, she works with diverse sets of stakeholders, from environmentalists to blockchain companies, who operate under the assumption that industry left alone will not act in the public interest. In 2016, she co-founded Code for Science and Society to systematize this work across the ecosystem of open source public interest technology.

It's clear that technology on its own is not enough to move technology in an ethical direction. To this end, she is currently a fellow at the Aspen Institute's Tech Policy Hub, where she is growing her skills to advocate for policies in the interest of digital human rights around the globe.

Formerly a data scientist, her work studying online political communication resulted in multiple peer-reviewed papers and press in outlets such as NPR and the Wall Street Journal.
