Karissa McKelvey is a public interest technologist and researcher working in solidarity with marginalized communities to defend their rights. Since 2013, she has focused on developing distributed ledger technology to re-engineer and re-imagine the Internet for the next generation. Karissa's perspectives and work has been featured in high-profile news outlets such as the New York Times, Wall Street Journal, and NPR.

Her work has been depended upon by at-risk users including environmental & human rights defenders, journalists, and civil society activists living within repressive environments. She combines her backgrounds in political science, complex systems research, and software engineering to deliver public interest initiatives that leverage emerging technologies.

She is an expert on cooperative and sustainable practices in open source. In 2016, she co-founded Code for Science and Society to systematize positive outcomes across the ecosystem of open source public interest technology. As a Founding Member of the Dat Protocol Foundation she helps govern the open source project in coordination with various working groups and partner organizations.

In 2019, she was a fellow at the Aspen Institute's Tech Policy Hub, where she grew her skills to advocate for ethical technology policy.
