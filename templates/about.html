Karissa McKelvey is a technology strategist and software engineer. She is a generalist who "connects the dots" between emerging technologies to build initiatives that center the public interest. She helps non-profit organizations, companies, and small projects reach better outcomes for universal access, security, privacy, inclusion, and equity. Karissa's work has been featured in high-profile news outlets such as the New York Times, Wall Street Journal, and NPR.

She is an expert on cooperative and sustainable practices in open source. In 2016, she co-founded Code for Science and Society to systemize positive outcomes across the ecosystem of open source public interest technology. As the Director of the Dat Foundation, she works with diverse sets of stakeholders who aim to build a better Web that values digital rights.

It's clear that technological solutions on their own are not enough to move the global Web in an ethical direction. To this end, she is currently a fellow at the Aspen Institute's Tech Policy Hub, where she is growing her skills to advocate for digital human rights around the globe.
